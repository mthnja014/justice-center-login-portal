Definition of Terms

AppealA legal process to ask a higher court to review a decision by judge in trial court because you believe that judge made a mistake.
ArrestSeize someone by legal authority and take them into custody.
Casecivil, criminal, and administrative matters in accordance with the rule of law.
Case trialA structured process where the facts of case are presented to a jury, and they decide if the defendent is guilty or not guilty of the charge offered.
Case hearingA brief court session that resolves a specific question before a full court trial takes place.
ComplainantA person or organization who give statement that something is unacceptable.
ConstableA police officer.
CourtAn institution with authority to adjudicate legal disputes between parties and carry out administration of justice in civil, criminal, and administrative matters in accordance with the rule of law.
Court RecordsDocuments constituting of the evidence of past court trials or hearings.
CrimeA behavior, either by act or commission, defined by statutory or common law as deserving of punishment or penalty.
Crime AnalystA person whose job is to use a set of quantitative and qualitative techniques to analyze data valuable to police agencies and their communities.
Crime Justice CenterRefers to jail or prison.
Crime RegistraAn official who evaluate data quality of the Provincial Quality Assurance.
DetectiveA police officer whose occupation is to investigate and solve ceimes.
DocketA list of cases for trial or people having cases pending.
GeoportalA type of web portal used to access geographic information system and other associated geographic services via the internet.
JudgeA public officer appointed to decide cases in the court of law.
JudgementA decision made by the court that has been entered into the public record.
LawyerA person whose job is to give advice to people about the law and speak for them in court.
Police StationA building which serves to accommodate police officers and other members of staff.
ProsecutorA barrister or other lawyer who conduct the case against a defendent(suspect) in a criminal court.
SentenceThe punishment a judge decides should be given to someone who has been convicted of crime.
SuspectA person thought to be guilty of crime or offence.
WCJS DirectorA person who is in charge of Western Cape Crime Services.
WitnessA person who saw crime happening.
